# About the Genus *Paralepidosteus* (Ginglymodi, Lepisosteidae) from the Gondwanan Cretaceous

**Authors:** Mireille Gayet & François J. Meunier
**Published:** Cybium 2001, 25(2): 153-159
**Original Language:** French

---

## Abstract

The authors justify maintaining the genus †*Paralepidosteus* for the species †*P. africanus* from the Santonian of Damergou, Niger. They propose to assign to this genus, albeit with some doubt (?†*Paralepidosteus* sp.), specimens from the Maastrichtian of Bolivia, Brazil, and India, as well as the species †*P. praecursor* from the Albian of Congo.

**Keywords:** Ginglymodi - Lepisosteidae - †*Paralepidosteus* - Africa - South America - India - Cretaceous

---

## Introduction

In 1943, Arambourg and Joleaud described Lepisosteidae remains (vertebrae and a dentary) from the Senonian of Damergou (Niger), which they assigned to a new genus †*Paralepidosteus* (†*P. africanus*). Casier (1961) created the species †*P. praecursor* for vertebrae from the Albian of the Molimba region (Congo); the author tentatively assigned scales and teeth found at Samba, a locality slightly south of the previous site, to this genus. Wenz (in Broin et al., 1974) reported the presence of lepisosteoid scales close to those of the genus †*Paralepidosteus* in the Coniacian-Santonian of In Beceten, Niger.

In 1976, Wiley assigned all these fossil remains to the extant genus *Atractosteus* (†*A. africanus*) and considered that the "dentary" described by Arambourg and Joleaud (1943) was actually an infraorbital.

Gayet and Meunier (1986) proposed a method for taxon recognition based on the pattern (size and density) of minute tubercles ornamenting the upper surface of the ganoine covering the scales. These tubercles, observed under scanning electron microscope, show different patterns according to groups (Lepisosteiformes, Polypteriformes, †Semionotiformes) and, within these groups, according to genera and even species.

The external surface of the scale ganoine was examined using scanning electron microscopy (SEM) following the method of Gayet and Meunier (1986). Morphometric characteristics of tubercles were measured on micrographs all taken at the same magnification.

## Results and Discussion

### Tubercle Analysis in Extant Lepisosteidae

The analysis of tubercle diameter and density on the ganoine surface of Lepisosteidae scales was first performed on extant forms. All species of both genera *Lepisosteus* and *Atractosteus*, males and females, juveniles and adults, as well as regenerated scales, were studied.

**The two extant genera separate clearly:**

### Key Finding: †*Paralepidosteus* is Distinct

The scales of †*Paralepidosteus praecursor* have a regular tubercular pattern like all Lepisosteidae, but the measurements differ notably:
- **Mean tubercle diameter:** 5.94 μm
- **Mean intertubercular space:** 6.00 μm

These measurements fall **outside the limits** of †*Obaichthys*, *Lepisosteus*, or *Atractosteus*.

### Evidence Against Wiley's Synonymy

In 1976, Wiley synonymized †*Paralepidosteus* with *Atractosteus*. However:

1. **None of the diagnostic characters of *Atractosteus*** defined by Wiley are observable in the Nigerian fossil
2. The fossil "differs from all other *Atractosteus* in the bony ridge pattern of the one preserved infraorbital" (Wiley 1976: 73)
3. The **vertebrae of †*P. africanus* are larger** than those of all *Atractosteus*
4. The **tubercle pattern** clearly separates †*Paralepidosteus* from both *Atractosteus* and *Lepisosteus*

### Williamson Canaliculi

The **presence of Williamson canaliculi** in the bony basal plate is characteristic of holostean scales (Lepisosteiformes and Semionotiformes) and **absent in Polypteriformes**.

## Conclusion

An identical taxon exists (based on ganoine tubercle morphology) in:
- Maastrichtian of Bolivia
- Albian of Congo
- Maastrichtian of India

The Bolivian taxon shares with †*P. africanus* from the Senonian of Niger the **very large size of its vertebrae**, but comparison based on scales cannot be made (scales unknown for †*P. africanus*).

### Final Taxonomic Decision

Rather than erecting a new genus for the Bolivian, Congolese, and Indian forms, the authors chose to assign these fossils to the genus †*Paralepidosteus* while maintaining some doubt due to the absence of scale comparisons for the type species.

**Final assignments:**
- †*Paralepidosteus africanus* (Niger) - **confirmed**
- ?†*P. praecursor* (Congo) - **tentative**
- ?†*Paralepidosteus* sp. (Bolivia and India) - **tentative**

### Paleobiogeographic Implications

The presence of the **same taxon across Gondwana** and the **absence of *Atractosteus*** on this supercontinent implies a **different paleobiogeography** than proposed by Wiley (1976) or Patterson (1981).
