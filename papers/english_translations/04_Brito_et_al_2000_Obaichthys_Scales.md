# The Morphology and Histology of the Scales of the Cretaceous Gar *Obaichthys* (Actinopterygii, Lepisosteidae): Phylogenetic Implications

**Authors:** Paulo M. Brito, François J. Meunier & Mireille Gayet
**Published:** C. R. Acad. Sci. Paris, Sciences de la Terre et des planètes / Earth and Planetary Sciences 331 (2000) 823-829
**Original Language:** English (with French abstract)

---

## Abstract

Scales of the lepisosteid fish †*Obaichthys decoratus* and †*O.? laevis*, from the Lower Cretaceous of the Araripe Plateau (Northeast Brazil) resemble those of palaeoniscoid-type, in which a ganoine layer overlies a dentin layer located above a basal plate composed of lamellar bone. However, the presence of canaliculi of Williamson in these scales confirms that †*Obaichthys* is a typical neopterygian. The scales of †*Obaichthys* differ from those of other gars, which do not have a dentin layer and that have ganoin tubercles with characteristic size and density. Our observations support the plesiomorphic status of †*Obaichthys* within the Lepisosteidae, and confirm the validity of the two species: †*O. decoratus* and †*O.? laevis*.

**Keywords:** Lepisosteidae / †*Obaichthys* / Early Cretaceous / scales / histology / phylogeny

## Introduction

### Lepisosteidae Overview

Lepisosteids or gars are a very particular neopterygian group, represented today by **seven species** and **two genera**: *Lepisosteus* and *Atractosteus*.

### Geographic and Temporal Distribution

- **Extant:** North and Central America, Caribbean
- **Past:** Widespread, especially Late Cretaceous and Early Tertiary
- **Stratigraphic range:** Mid Cretaceous to Present

### †*Obaichthys*: The Oldest Lepisosteid

Discovered in the early 1990s in the Albian Romualdo Member of the Santana Formation:
- **†*Obaichthys decoratus***: Nearly complete specimen
- **†*O.? laevis***: Known only by the anterior part of the body

†*Obaichthys* possesses many lepisosteid synapomorphies BUT differs in:
- Absence of plicidentine
- Presence of a free maxilla
- Structure of the postorbital region

**Conclusion:** †*Obaichthys* forms the **sister group to all other lepisosteids**

**Palaeonisciformes:**
- †*Aeduella* sp. - France

1. **SEM observation** of tubercles on external ganoine surface

#### Species-Specific Differences

**†*O.? laevis*:**
- Entire surface covered by ganoine
- Dentine developed only in **posterior half** of scales
- Dentine organized around single stratum of vascular regions
- Each vascular area with surrounding dentine = "**odontocomplex**"

**†*O. decoratus*:**
- Ganoine limited to external ridges only
- Dentine situated only beneath thickened ganoine areas

## Discussion

### Williamson Canaliculi

The presence of Williamson canaliculi:
- **ABSENT** in palaeoniscoids and polypterids
- **PRESENT** in †*Obaichthys*

This confirms †*Obaichthys* exclusion from "Palaeonisciformes" and Polypteriformes, and confirms its placement within **Neopterygii**.

## Conclusions

1. **Dentine presence** between stratified ganoine and bony basal plate (palaeoniscoid-type scale) is the plesiomorphic condition for actinopterygians; this condition, found in †*Obaichthys decoratus* and †*O.? laevis*, **strongly confirms these species as the plesiomorphic sister group of all other gars**.

2. Despite three superimposed layers (as in palaeoniscoid-type scales), the presence of **Williamson canaliculi** confirms †*Obaichthys* as a **typical neopterygian**.

3. The presence of dentine on **only the posterior half** of scales in †*O.? laevis* shows a **gradual transition** from palaeoniscoid to lepisosteoid scale type.

4. Except for †*Obaichthys*, the histological scale pattern is **very similar** in all other fossil and extant gars and is therefore **not useful** for taxonomic diagnosis.

5. **Size and distance between tubercles** on the external ganoine surface are confirmed as being of **taxonomic interest** for distinguishing different gar genera.

6. The presence of palaeoniscoid-type scales, supposed absence of odontodes, and characteristic tubercle size/distance confirm that **†*Obaichthys decoratus* and †*O.? laevis* form a clade**.
